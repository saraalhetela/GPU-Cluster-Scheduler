The two things worth saying plainly

Both matter more to credibility than the headline number itself.

Real vs. synthetic data

Real (Alibaba PAI) 85% Synthetic random 10% Synthetic edge-case 5%

170 of 200 jobs are real Alibaba PAI GPU-cluster trace data (NSDI '22). The rest is synthetic: 20 ordinary random jobs, 10 forced tight-deadline/high-priority jobs so the reward function's penalty terms are demonstrably exercised.

Not real: deadline and priority — neither trace logs an SLA or priority field. Both are principled proxies, derived from feasibility slack and a hashed grouping field.

Scope: what this agent does NOT do

  • Trains under a real but approximate scarcity signal — a worst-case static proxy, not a live multi-agent simulation.
  • Does not know about other specific competing jobs, and does not learn to yield GPUs to a more urgent one.
  • When jobs compete for the same hour, an earliest-deadline-first rule arbitrates — a necessary bolt-on, not something the agent learned.
  • True joint / multi-agent scheduling is explicitly out of scope.
